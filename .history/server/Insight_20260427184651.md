Player Behavior Insights – LILA Player Journey Tool

This document highlights key behavioral patterns observed from gameplay telemetry data using the visualization tool.

Each insight is backed by observable patterns in movement, event density, and spatial clustering.

🔍 Insight 1: Combat is heavily concentrated in limited map zones
Observation

Kill events are not evenly distributed across the map. Instead, they form dense clusters in specific regions, while large map areas show almost no combat activity.

Evidence
Kill heatmap shows repeated overlapping red zones in same coordinates
Multiple matches show identical combat hotspots
Bot and human kills both follow same spatial clustering pattern
Interpretation

Players naturally converge to a few high-value engagement zones rather than exploring the full map.

Actionable Impact
Level designers can rebalance spawn points or objectives
Introduce new POIs in low-combat zones to improve map usage
Adjust reward density to distribute player movement more evenly
 Insight 2: Loot drives predictable player rotation paths
Observation

Loot events form linear and repeating paths that players follow across matches.

Evidence
Green loot markers form corridor-like patterns
Movement lines align with loot clusters
Multiple players follow similar traversal routes
Interpretation

Players are optimizing routes for loot efficiency, leading to predictable movement behavior.

Actionable Impact
Introduce randomized loot placements to reduce predictability
Create dynamic loot spawns to force adaptive movement
Encourage strategic exploration rather than linear routing
 Insight 3: Large portions of maps are underutilized
Observation

Significant areas of the map show minimal or zero player activity across multiple matches.

Evidence
Sparse or absent movement traces in outer map regions
Heatmaps show strong centralization of activity
No significant kill/loot/death events in certain zones
Interpretation

Map design is encouraging central clustering, leaving peripheral areas irrelevant.

Actionable Impact
Add strategic objectives or loot in unused zones
Improve traversal incentives (cover, shortcuts, events)
Redesign weak zones into meaningful gameplay areas
 Insight 4: Human vs Bot engagement patterns differ subtly
Observation

Human players show more non-linear movement, while bots follow more structured paths.

Evidence
Human movement paths are irregular and exploratory
Bot paths appear more linear and repetitive
Bots die more consistently in high-combat zones
Interpretation

Bots lack adaptive navigation behavior compared to humans, making them predictable targets.

Actionable Impact
Improve bot pathfinding logic for realism
Introduce randomness in bot decision-making
Balance bot placement to avoid easy exploitation
 Insight 5: Early-game engagement spike
Observation

Most kills occur early in match timeline, shortly after spawn.

Evidence
Timeline slider shows kill density concentrated in early timestamps
Later match phases show reduced combat frequency
Loot and movement stabilize mid-match
Interpretation

Early game is the most aggressive phase, possibly due to spawn proximity overlap.

Actionable Impact
Adjust spawn distribution to reduce early collisions
Introduce spawn protection or separation logic
Create mid-game objectives to sustain engagement
 Summary

The data reveals three core behavioral trends:

Players converge into predictable combat zones
Loot strongly dictates movement patterns
Large map areas are underutilized
 Final Takeaway

This tool demonstrates that player behavior is not random, but highly influenced by:

Map layout
Loot distribution
Spawn logic

These insights can directly guide:

Map redesign
Gameplay balancing
Bot AI improvements
